# Chord playback and timeline

> Play along with a synced chord chart, change the capo, and slow down or loop the tricky bars.

## Overview

When an analysis finishes, FRELODY shows the playback view. This is where the loop pays off: the chords scroll in time with the song, so you can follow along, slow it down, and loop the hard parts until they're under your fingers.

Across the top you'll find meta chips: the play **key** (capo-aware, shown as "sounds in <key>"), the **BPM** to one decimal place, and the **time signature**. A beat strip of dots highlights the active beat in each measure, so you always know where you are in the bar.

The chord grid is the centre of it all. As the song plays, FRELODY highlights the active chord and beat, and you can tap any chord to jump straight to that point. Cells marked **N** or **X** are rests with no diagram; they're kept on purpose to hold the rhythm and spacing.

The player itself floats in a draggable window you can move out of the way or minimise; on small screens it docks neatly at the bottom instead.

## Using the capo and chord shapes

The **Capo** stepper lets you change the shapes you play without changing how the song sounds. It starts at "Off" and steps up to fret 11, with a reset button. As you raise the capo, FRELODY shows easier shapes to finger, with a hint like "Showing shapes to finger with a capo on fret N — the recording still sounds in <key>."

The collapsible **Chord shapes** section lists every unique chord in the song as a diagram, and those diagrams update with the capo too. It's a quick reference while you learn.

## Tips

- Tap a chord in the grid to seek the song to that moment and rehearse it.
- Slow the tempo down and loop a single tricky section to drill it before playing at full speed.
- Drag the floating player aside, or minimise it, to keep the chord grid in full view.
