# What Discover does

> Discover is where you find a song and start turning it into a playable chord chart.

## Overview

**Discover** is the heart of FRELODY. Paste a link or search for a song, and FRELODY detects the chords, lines them up with the lyrics, and gets you ready to play along. It is the first step in the loop: paste a link, get chords and lyrics, slow it down, loop the tricky bars, play.

Discover opens at `/discover`, and the home page (`/`) is the same search experience in landing mode. At the top you choose your source with a toggle between **YouTube** and **TikTok**. Your choice is remembered for next time. The YouTube view greets you with "Find songs. Start playing."; the TikTok view says "Hear a sound. Learn the chords."

The search box adapts to the source. On YouTube you can type a song title, an artist, or paste a YouTube URL, and quick suggestion chips like worship songs, fingerstyle guitar, Ed Sheeran, jazz standards and hillsong help you explore. TikTok has no public search, so you paste a TikTok link instead. Either way, pasting a TikTok URL sends you straight to the TikTok lane.

Results appear in a grid. If nothing matches you'll see "No results found", and while you're still deciding a scrolling chord display keeps the page alive. Pick a result and FRELODY takes you to the analysis page for that video, where the chords are worked out.

## Tips

- Use the **YouTube** / **TikTok** toggle to switch sources; your last choice is saved.
- Paste a full link straight into the search box, or just type an artist or song title on YouTube.
- A pasted TikTok link always routes to the TikTok lane, whichever toggle is selected.
