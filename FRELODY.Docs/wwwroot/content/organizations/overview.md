# Organizations overview

> Understand what an organization is in FRELODY, how to tell if you belong to one, and how roles fit together.

## Overview

An organization is a shared workspace for a team's music — a band, church or group working on songs together. When you belong to one, your songs, playlists and songbooks sit inside that shared space.

Open `/organizations/me` to see your organization. If you are a member, the page shows the organization's name, industry, member count, country and the date it was created ("Since <date>"). If you do not belong to one yet, you will see "You don't belong to an organization", with options to **Create organization** or **Join with ID**.

Members hold a role that sets what they can do. From most to least access, the roles are **Owner**, **Admin**, **Manager**, **Editor**, **Contributor**, **Viewer** and **Guest**. Owners and Admins manage the organization; Editors and Contributors create content; Viewers and Guests have lighter access.

## Steps

1. Open `/organizations/me`.
2. If you are a member, review the organization name, industry, member count, country and "Since" date.
3. If you do not belong to one, choose **Create organization** to start your own, or **Join with ID** to join an existing one.

## Tips

- You can belong to one active organization at a time; joining another switches your active workspace.
- Your role decides what you can manage — see Members, roles and ranks for the full breakdown.
