# How FRELODY works

> See how a pasted link becomes a playable, slow-it-down chord chart in three steps.

## Overview

FRELODY takes a song link and gives you back chords you can play. There is no special software to install and no music theory required to get started. You paste a link, FRELODY does the listening, and you get a chart lined up with the audio.

Under the bonnet, FRELODY detects the chords in the recording, works out the key, tempo and time signature, and syncs each chord to the moment it is played. That is what lets you slow the song down and loop a single bar without losing your place.

## The three steps

1. **Paste a link.** Drop in any YouTube or TikTok song from the **Discover** search.
2. **Get the chords.** FRELODY detects the chords and lines them up with the audio.
3. **Slow it down and loop.** Drop the tempo, loop the hard bar, and play along.

## What the playback view shows

When analysis finishes, the playback view gives you a synced chord grid, a beat strip that highlights the active beat, and chips showing the key, BPM and time signature. A **Capo** stepper lets you switch to easier shapes, and a chord-shapes section shows a diagram for every chord in the song.

## Tips

- Tap any chord in the grid to jump straight to that moment in the song.
- Rest cells marked N or X are kept on purpose, so the rhythm and spacing stay accurate.
