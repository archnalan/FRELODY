# How to use this guide

> Learn how this documentation is organised so you can find what you need quickly.

## Overview

This guide is the handbook for FRELODY. It explains how to analyse songs, build chord charts, organise your library and manage your account, with each page focused on a single task or topic.

You do not need to read it cover to cover. Most people land here from a question, find the page that answers it, and get back to playing. If you are brand new, start with [Welcome to FRELODY](/docs/getting-started/welcome) and [How FRELODY works](/docs/getting-started/how-frelody-works), then dip into the rest as you need it.

## How it is organised

The guide is grouped into 11 sections, ordered roughly from first steps to advanced and back-office topics:

- **Getting started** — what FRELODY is, creating an account, signing in, and display options.
- **Discover** — pasting links, analysing YouTube and TikTok songs, playback, and free limits.
- **Compose** — the song editor, importing, editing sections, and chord charts.
- **Library** — your songs, playlists and songbooks.
- **Pricing** — plans, billing, and how premium access works.
- **Printing** — printing and exporting chord charts.
- **Sharing** — share links, public pages, and social cards.
- **Account** — your profile, settings, and security.
- **Organizations** — shared team workspaces, members and roles.
- **Administration** — dashboards, members, tenants, and plan management.
- **Help** — this guide, the FAQ, the glossary, and support.

## Finding your way around

1. Use the navigation menu to jump between sections and pages.
2. Use the search box to look up a feature or term by name.
3. Follow the **Related pages** links at the bottom of each page to dig deeper.
4. Use [Download this guide](/docs/printing/download-this-guide) if you would rather read offline or print a copy.

## Tips

- Each page opens with a one-line summary so you can tell at a glance whether it is the one you want.
- Some pages cover paid features. They explain the benefit and link to [Plans and billing](/docs/pricing/plans), but you can read them whether or not you have a subscription.
