# Create and edit chord charts

> Build a custom chord chart step by step — either drawn by hand or uploaded as an image.

## Overview

A chord chart pairs a chord with a diagram of how to play it. FRELODY guides you through making one with a short wizard: the heading reads "Create" or "Edit" "Chord Chart", with the subtitle "Follow the steps to create your custom chord chart", and a **Back** button to step out.

You choose where the diagram comes from with a source toggle: **Draw it** (recommended) opens the interactive editor, while **Upload image** lets you bring your own PNG, JPG or SVG. When you're editing an existing chart, the source toggle is locked to whatever it was created with.

The wizard runs through five steps — **Details**, **Draw**, **Media**, **Notes** and **Review** — so you fill in the chord's information, create or attach its diagram, add any media and notes, and check it all before saving. This is a member feature.

## Steps

1. Start a new chart, or open an existing one to edit it.
2. On the source toggle, pick **Draw it** to use the interactive editor, or **Upload image** to use your own PNG, JPG or SVG.
3. **Details** — fill in the chord's information.
4. **Draw** — draw the diagram, or attach your uploaded image.
5. **Media** — add any supporting media.
6. **Notes** — add notes about the shape or fingering.
7. **Review** — check everything, then save the chart.

## Tips

- **Draw it** is the recommended route — it gives you a consistent, scalable diagram without leaving FRELODY.
- The source can't be changed once a chart exists, so pick **Draw it** or **Upload image** thoughtfully on a new chart.
