# Draw a chord (chord-draw)

> Use the free, interactive fret-diagram tool at `/chord-draw` to draw a chord shape and download it.

## Overview

`/chord-draw` is FRELODY's standalone chord diagram editor — the heading reads "Chord diagram editor" — and anyone can use it, signed in or not. It's the quickest way to sketch a fingering and save a clean diagram.

The tool tells you how it works right at the top: "Tap to place a finger, drag across a fret for a barre, tap O/× above a string to mark it open or muted." You build the shape directly on the fretboard, and the editor opens with its settings panel ready so you can adjust the diagram as you go.

When your chart looks right, you can save it with **Download SVG** or **Download PNG**. Both buttons stay disabled until there's a chart to export. The canvas starts from a sensible default, so you always have something to build on.

## Steps

1. Open `/chord-draw`.
2. Tap the fretboard to place a finger.
3. Drag across a fret to add a barre.
4. Tap **O** or **×** above a string to mark it open or muted.
5. Adjust the diagram using the settings panel.
6. Choose **Download SVG** or **Download PNG** to save your chord.

## Tips

- SVG keeps the diagram crisp at any size; PNG is handy for pasting straight into other apps.
- The download buttons only light up once there's a shape on the board — place at least one marker first.
