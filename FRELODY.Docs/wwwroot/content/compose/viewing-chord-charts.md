# Viewing a chord chart

> See a single chord chart's details and diagram on its own page.

## Overview

Every chord chart has a detail page, headed "Chord Chart Details", where you can read it on its own. It's a public view, so you don't need to be signed in to look at a chart someone has shared.

The page lays out the essentials in simple rows: **Chord:** the chord it's for, **Fret:** the fret position, and **Diagram:** the shape itself. When the chart was drawn or is a standard shape, the diagram renders right there. A **Back** button returns you to where you came from.

This is a read-only view — to change a chart, open it in the chord chart editor instead.

## Tips

- The **Fret** row tells you where the shape sits on the neck — handy for chords played higher up.
- To edit what you're viewing, open the chart in the editor from [Create and edit chord charts](/docs/compose/chord-charts).
