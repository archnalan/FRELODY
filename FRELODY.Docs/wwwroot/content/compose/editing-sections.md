# Editing sections and rows

> Shape a song in Compose by adding, arranging and reordering its sections, rows and segments.

## Overview

In Compose a song is built from sections — think verse, chorus, bridge — and each section holds rows, which in turn hold segments. Segments are the small building blocks where your lyrics and chords live. Together they give the chart its shape.

You add segments with **Add segment** and arrange them by dragging; a drop indicator shows where a segment will land as you move it. Each row also has its own actions for the common edits you'll reach for while you work.

As you go, a preview shows how the song reads, so you can keep an eye on the whole chart while you rearrange the parts. This is a member feature inside the Compose editor.

## Steps

1. Open your song in Compose.
2. Select the section you want to edit, using the tabbed section boards.
3. Use **Add segment** to add a new segment to a row.
4. Drag a segment to reorder it — the drop indicator shows where it will go.
5. Use the per-row actions for other changes to a row.
6. Check the preview to see how the section reads, then **Save Song**.

## Tips

- Keep sections short and named the way you'd call them out while playing — it makes the chart easier to follow.
- Reorder by dragging rather than retyping; the drop indicator removes the guesswork.
