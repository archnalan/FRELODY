# Saving and session recovery

> Save your work in Compose and bring back unsaved changes if a session ends before you're done.

## Overview

The **Save Song** button on the **Song Editor** persists your song. Save often — it's the surest way to keep your work.

Compose also looks after you between saves. If you leave with unsaved changes, the editor keeps a backup. When you come back and there's something to restore, it shows a notice: "Session Recovery: Unsaved changes detected. Would you like to restore your previous work?" with **Preview**, **Restore** and **Discard** buttons. **Restore** is offered when you're creating a new song.

Your auto-saved drafts also live in your account. The **Recovery** tab on your profile lists "Recovery Items" — "Automatically saved drafts from your editing sessions" — with timestamps and a **Preview** action, so you can find earlier work even after you've moved on.

This is a member feature inside Compose.

## Steps

1. While editing in Compose, hit **Save Song** to persist your song.
2. If you return and see the Session Recovery notice, choose **Preview** to check the backup first.
3. Choose **Restore** to bring the unsaved work back into the editor, or **Discard** to drop it.
4. To find older drafts, open the **Recovery** tab on your profile and use **Preview** on a Recovery Item.

## Tips

- Always **Preview** before you **Restore** so you know exactly what's coming back.
- Recovery is a safety net, not a substitute for saving — hit **Save Song** whenever you reach a good stopping point.
