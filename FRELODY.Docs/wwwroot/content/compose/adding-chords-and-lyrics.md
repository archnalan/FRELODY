# Adding chords and lyrics

> Attach chords to your lyric segments in Compose and keep them in tune with the song's key.

## Overview

Lyrics and chords meet at the segment level in Compose. Each segment can carry a chord, so the chart shows exactly where a chord change falls against the words — the way you'd read it while playing.

You attach a chord to a segment with the chord dropdown, and you can customise it when you need a shape that isn't in the standard list. The chords you add work alongside the key and transpose controls on the **Song Editor**, so changing the working key moves your chords with it.

This is a member feature inside the Compose editor, and it's the step that turns a sheet of lyrics into a playable chart.

## Steps

1. Open your song in Compose and select the segment where the chord changes.
2. Open the chord dropdown for that segment.
3. Pick the chord you want, or use the customise option for a non-standard shape.
4. Repeat across the song so each chord sits above the right word.
5. Use the original and working key controls, with the transpose button, to shift the whole song if you need a different key.
6. Hit **Save Song** when you're happy.

## Tips

- Place a chord on the segment where the change actually happens — that's what keeps the chart honest.
- Set your working key first; transposing afterwards keeps every chord consistent.
