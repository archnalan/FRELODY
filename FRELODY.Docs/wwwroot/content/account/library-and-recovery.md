# Library and recovery

> Browse the content saved to your profile and restore drafts FRELODY saved automatically while you were editing.

## Overview

Your profile has two tabs for finding your work: **Library** and **Recovery**. Open your profile at `/user-profile`, then choose the tab you need.

The **Library** tab lists the content tied to your account, so you can jump straight back to a song you saved. The **Recovery** tab lists **Recovery Items** — "Automatically saved drafts from your editing sessions". Each item shows a timestamp and a **Preview** action.

Recovery is your safety net. If you were working in Compose and a session ended before you saved, FRELODY keeps a draft so your work is not lost. This is the same mechanism behind the session-recovery prompt in the editor.

## Steps

1. Open `/user-profile`.
2. Select the **Library** tab to see content saved to your account.
3. Select the **Recovery** tab to see your auto-saved drafts.
4. Find the **Recovery Item** you want by its timestamp.
5. Select **Preview** to review that draft before you continue working on it.

## Tips

- Recovery drafts are saved automatically as you work in Compose — you do not need to do anything to create them.
- A timestamp helps you pick the most recent draft when there are several.
- To turn a recovered draft into a permanent song, open it and save it from the editor.
