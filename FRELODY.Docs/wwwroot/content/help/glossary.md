# Glossary

> Plain-English definitions of the terms you will meet across FRELODY and this guide.

## Overview

This is a quick reference for the words used throughout FRELODY and this documentation. If a term on another page is unfamiliar, look it up here. The entries are grouped by where you will most often run into them.

## Playing and practising

- **Analysis / analysed song** — the result of FRELODY listening to a link and producing a chord chart. Each new analysis counts toward your free daily limit.
- **Synced chords** — chords lined up with the exact moments they are played in the recording.
- **Beat strip** — the row of dots that highlights the active beat within each bar during playback.
- **Capo** — a clamp on the guitar neck. FRELODY's capo control shows easier chord shapes while the recording still sounds in its original key.
- **Key and transpose** — the key is the song's tonal home; transposing shifts every chord up or down so the song suits your voice or instrument.
- **Today's Songs** — the songs you have unlocked today, free to replay for 24 hours.

## Building charts

- **Chord chart** — a diagram showing where to place your fingers for a chord.
- **Chord-draw** — the editor for drawing your own chord diagrams.
- **Compose** — FRELODY's song editor for building and editing songs.
- **Songbook** — a collection that groups songs by book, artist, album or category.
- **Playlist** — a personal collection of songs you put together.

## Accounts and teams

- **Organization** — a shared workspace for a team's music, with members and roles.
- **Tenant** — an application-level grouping used by administrators.
- **Billing status and period** — your subscription state (such as trial, recurring or lifetime) and how often it renews.

## Sharing

- **Share link** — a link that lets others open a song or playlist you have shared.
- **OG card** — the preview image and text that appear when a share link is posted to chat or social apps.
